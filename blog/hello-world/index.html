Wakaful Combines Takaful and Smart Contracts

Wakaful is experimenting on combining elements of Takaful applications on the blockchain. it introduces the element of Wakf with a vision is to make Takaful products more accessible. It uses smart contracts built on the Ethereum blockchain to improve governance, transparency and trust in the Islamic insurance sector.

Wakaful made their first public appearance at the Takaful Rendezvous 2018. One key take-away from the event was that the Insurance sector as a whole (inclusive of takaful) is sharing the problems of the Banking world, that the financial and insurance products that were offered are not accessible and affordable to the bottom 40%.

Potential Use-cases for Takaful on the Blockchain

Some of the use cases for insurance on the blockchains are parametric insurance [cohn, west, parker] as it has the potential to improve business processes, performance management, reporting, and contract execution without having to discard existing IT systems. According to Swiss Re, Parametric insurance is a type of insurance that is triggered by things such as natural disasters or flight delays. Triggers such as earthquakes and tsunamis or even rain can trigger smart contracts to automate payouts in an orderly fashion.
