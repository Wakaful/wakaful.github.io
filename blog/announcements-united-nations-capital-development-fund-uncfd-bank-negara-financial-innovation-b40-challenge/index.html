Wakaful Accepted into Financial Innovation Lab B40 Challenge

We are delighted to announce that Wakaful has been accepted into the United Nations Capital Development Fund (UNCDF) Financial Innovation Lab B40 Challenge as one of the top 20 applicants. Wakaful will participate for the 5 day boot-camp held at Orbit Fintech Hub and is in the running for a USD 100,000 grant.

The teams were selected based on four categories, namely the impact, practicality, interoperability, and lastly uniqueness and creativity.

The Financial Innovation Lab is an initiative of the UN Capital Development Fund (UNCDF) and the Metlife Foundation. The Lab was set up to find innovative solutions in Financial technology to make digital financial services accessible for low - middle income people in Africa, Asia, and the Pacific. UNCDF has partnered with Bank Negara Malaysia, The Malaysian Digital Economy Corporation (MDEC) and the Metlife Foundation

How Will Wakaful Benefit the Bottom 40%

Only 5% of low-income consumers in emerging economies are currently using micro-insurance products. This presents a problem as insurance and takaful coverage may not be so inclusive. Wakaful aims to make insurance accessible by working with takaful partners to use Wakf, (a social benevolent vehicle) to make insurance more affordable. The intended result is to be able to help make insurance for individuals at very low costs.
